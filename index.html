Quaid-e-Azam


Quaid-e-Azam (meaning "Great Leader") is the title given to Muhammad Ali Jinnah (1876–1948), the founder and first Governor-General of Pakistan. He is revered as the Baba-e-Qaum ("Father of the Nation") for his role in leading the Pakistan Movement to secure an independent state for Muslims in the South Asian subcontinent
